# Kutup Trademark Policy

This policy explains how the kutup name, logo, and other brand assets
may be used. It exists alongside — and is separate from — the AGPL
license that covers kutup's source code.

## What this policy covers

Collectively referred to in this document as the **"kutup Marks"**:

- The name **"Kutup"** and the organization name **"kutupbulut"**, in
  any capitalization or stylization.
- The **three-diamond mark** in any rendering — including the inline
  SVG in `frontend/src/components/KutupLogo.tsx`, the source SVG at
  `src-tauri/icons/source.svg`, and the platform-specific icon set
  rendered from it (the desktop `.icns` / `.ico` / `.png` files in
  `src-tauri/icons/`, the iOS `AppIcon.appiconset`, the Android
  `mipmap-*` assets, and the Windows `Square*Logo.png` tiles).
- Any wordmark, logotype, or composite mark that combines the name
  with the three-diamond mark.
- Any future logo artwork, wordmarks, or brand assets committed to
  this repository.

## Why this policy exists

Kutup is an end-to-end encrypted product. Users decide to trust kutup
with their files in part because of what the kutup name and brand
stand for. If anyone could ship a backdoored fork under the kutup
name, that trust would be misplaced and the project's reputation would
be the casualty. This policy is the cheap-and-effective protection
that lets us guard the brand without requiring you to register
trademarks per jurisdiction.

The AGPL covers the **code**. The AGPL is intentionally silent on
trademarks; this policy fills that gap.

## Relationship to the AGPL code license

The AGPL grants you broad rights to use, modify, and redistribute the
kutup source code. **It does not grant any right to use the kutup
Marks.** You can fork the code, run it, modify it, and distribute your
modifications under the AGPL — what you can't do, without permission,
is keep calling that fork "Kutup" or keep using the kutup logo. The
two licenses are independent.

This is the same arrangement Mozilla, Bitwarden, Tauri, and Nextcloud
use, and it's why projects like Iceweasel (Debian's rebuilt Firefox)
and Vaultwarden (a Bitwarden-compatible server) exist under different
names.

## Uses that do not require prior permission

You are welcome to do these without contacting us. Attribution
("Kutup is a trademark of Alperen Albayrak") is appreciated but
not required.

- **Truthful references.** Articles, blog posts, tutorials, talks,
  comparison tables, academic papers, and reviews that discuss kutup
  by name.
- **"Works with kutup" / "Integrates with kutup."** If your software
  genuinely interoperates with kutup, say so.
- **Linking.** Linking to the kutup repository, releases page, or
  project website.
- **Screenshots.** Screenshots of kutup that include the logo, used to
  illustrate articles or documentation.
- **"Works with kutup" badges** for community-maintained integrations
  (use the unmodified logo at reasonable size).
- **Personal use.** Stickers, T-shirts, or other items you make for
  yourself, friends, or a non-commercial meetup — not for sale.

## Uses that require written permission

Please email us first.

- **Merchandise or services for sale** that prominently feature the
  kutup name or logo.
- **Distributing a fork** of kutup that uses the kutup name or the
  kutup logo. (You may distribute a fork under a different name and
  with the marks removed — that's what the AGPL covers.)
- **Domain names** containing "kutup" or "kutupbulut" that are not
  clearly community or fan sites.
- **Company names, product names, or app-store listings** that include
  the kutup Marks.
- **Endorsement or partnership claims.** Any wording or visual that
  suggests kutup endorses, sponsors, or is affiliated with you, unless
  that's actually the case.

## Uses that are never permitted

These would not be granted permission.

- **Modified, recolored, or derivative logos** that could be confused
  with the kutup mark. You may scale the logo; that's all.
- **False endorsement.** Any use that misleads users about an
  affiliation, partnership, or audit relationship.
- **Security claims we haven't made.** Because kutup is an E2EE
  product, claims like "audited by kutup" or "kutup-certified secure"
  are particularly damaging when false. Don't make them.
- **Disparaging or defamatory contexts.** Using the kutup Marks in
  ways that damage the project's reputation.
- **Hate, harassment, or illegal activity.** Using the kutup Marks in
  service of any of these.

## Forks must rebrand

If you distribute a modified version of kutup, you must **remove or
replace the kutup name and the kutup Marks** from your fork before
distribution. The AGPL gives you the code; this policy is what does
and does not grant the brand.

You may, of course, describe your fork's origin truthfully ("based on
kutup", "derived from kutup") — that's the "truthful references"
permission above. What you can't do is keep calling it "Kutup" or use
the three-diamond mark as your own.

## Attribution format

When attribution is appropriate (academic papers, distribution
notices, longer articles), the conventional format is:

> *Kutup is a trademark of Alperen Albayrak.*

The trademark symbol (™) may be used; the registered symbol (®) may
**not** — kutup has no registered trademark at the time of this
policy.

## Contact

For all brand-related requests — permission, clarification, takedown,
or anything else this policy doesn't cover — email **`dev@kutup.dev`**
or open an issue on the kutup repository with the `[brand]` tag.
